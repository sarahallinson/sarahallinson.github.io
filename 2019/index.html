From the first issue of Integral Functions, e-zine published January 15, 1996.

Archived at textfiles.com

I don't really like the idea of 'target markets', but here's a list of a few attributes from what I assume/​predict will be our 'average' reader: - computer literacy attained before puberty - semi/full working knowledge of 1-3 computer languages - interested in fiction, poetry, and/or art (outside of school) - thinks about the future more than the past or present - has more than 1 year experience with BBS/Internet culture - hearing the term 'information-highway' causes sudden vomiting The above list is not an attempt to generalize or limit our potential readers. Please glance through the 'zine and decide for yourself whether it fits your tastes or not. You'll notice that the identities of both the staff and those that contribute to Integral Functions will - for the most part - remain anonymous. In 'cyberspace', it doesn't matter who you are...​only what you say makes a difference. Reputations can be formatted and re-initialized. Identity is nullified.
